Why it matters — by who's asking

The stakes change depending on where you sit.

A half-star slide can wipe out your Quality Bonus Payment — and you'd learn it a year too late.

Cut points move every year — a measure comfortably above threshold in January can slip below it by the time CMS scores you, with nothing left to work. The plans that hold their rating watch performance against the cut point during the year, while members can still be moved.

How OneDash answers it

Measure-level performance against live cut points — not a year-end surprise.

OneDash works alongside your certified CMS/HEDIS reporting vendor — a real-time operational layer that sharpens the numbers you already report, not a replacement for them.

  • Real-time cut-point trackingDaily feeds re-score every clinical Stars and HEDIS measure against current thresholds — so you see half-star risk while you can still close it. Real-time, not just faster reporting.
  • Refined, real-time denominatorsCleans up who's actually in each measure and who's already met it — so your team stops chasing gaps that are already closed.

Three triple-weighted adherence measures decide your Part D score. Missing one isn't a rounding error.

Diabetes, hypertension, and cholesterol adherence carry triple weight — any one can pull your summary rating down. And CMR isn't optional: you have to hit the rate and prove it. By hand, in Q4, that burns out pharmacists and still falls short.

How OneDash answers it

Hit the heavy-weighted measures — and prove CMR compliance — without the manual scramble.

  • CMR completion, trackedMembers' CMR appointments booked automatically with your pharmacists — completion tracked and documented inside OneDash.
  • Automated adherence outreachOutreach fires automatically before a member lapses — so your pharmacists work the exceptions, not the whole panel.

Your MLR and your Star Rating set your AEP position. Both erode when gaps close late.

Late gap closure hits your books twice — avoidable medical spend that pushes MLR the wrong way, and forfeited Quality Bonus revenue when the rating slips. Walk into AEP soft on both and you're defending share, not taking it.
