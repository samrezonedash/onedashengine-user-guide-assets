Why it matters — by who's asking

The stakes change depending on where you sit.

A half-star slide can wipe out your Quality Bonus Payment — and you'd learn it a year too late.

Cut points move every year, and a measure that was comfortably above threshold in January can fall below it by the time CMS calculates your rating. By then there's nothing left to work. The plans that hold their rating are the ones watching measure-level performance — and coordinating care — against the cut point during the measurement year, while members can still be moved.

How OneDash answers it

Measure-level performance against live cut points — not a year-end surprise.

OneDash works alongside your certified CMS/HEDIS reporting vendor — a real-time operational layer that sharpens the numbers you already report, not a replacement for them.

  • Real-time cut-point trackingDaily data feeds re-score every clinical Stars and HEDIS measure — cancer screenings, controlling blood pressure, diabetes care, OMW — against current thresholds every day, so you see the half-star risk while you can still close it. Real-time, backed by daily ingestion — not just faster reporting.
  • Refined, real-time denominatorsSits on top of your reporting vendor and cleans up who's actually in each measure and who's already met it. For OMW, we flag women who filled a qualifying osteoporosis medication within 24 hours and close the gap automatically — so your team stops chasing gaps that are already met and focuses on who truly needs outreach.
  • Members who can still flipTargeted lists of the exact members who move a measure above threshold — ranked by impact on your rating.

Three triple-weighted adherence measures decide your Part D score. Missing one isn't a rounding error.

Diabetes, hypertension, and cholesterol adherence carry triple weight — they can single-handedly pull your summary rating down. And CMR completion isn't optional: you have to hit the rate and prove it. Doing that by hand, member by member, in the last quarter, is how plans burn out their pharmacists and still fall short.

How OneDash answers it

Hit the heavy-weighted measures — and prove CMR compliance — without the manual scramble.

  • PDC tracked per member, per measureMAD, MAH, and MAC adherence monitored continuously, with automated refill and outreach sequences before a member falls below 80%.
  • CMR completion, trackedAI agents call members to schedule their CMR appointments with your pharmacists — and completion is tracked and documented inside OneDash.
  • Automated adherence outreachAI calls, texts, and provider faxes fire on schedule — so your pharmacists work the exceptions, not the whole panel.

Your MLR and your Star Rating set your AEP position. Both erode when gaps close late.

Late gap closure shows up twice on your books: as avoidable medical spend that pushes your medical-loss ratio the wrong way, and as forfeited Quality Bonus revenue when the rating slips. Go into AEP with a soft rating and a strained cost structure and you're defending share, not taking it. The question isn't whether to invest in quality — it's whether the investment returns.

How OneDash answers it
